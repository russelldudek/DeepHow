# DeepHow Brand Intelligence

## Employer identity
- Official company website: https://deephow.com/
- Official logo asset discovered in the current website navigation: https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a896806364451d3e31efdb/68a8a17242ec643c737759bd_deephow-logo.svg
- The unmodified official SVG is expected at `assets/brand/deephow-logo.svg`. The publication workflow retrieves the exact source asset; the HTML contains an accessible text fallback only when the image cannot load.
- Use is nominative: the campaign identifies the target employer and remains visibly labeled as an independent Russell Dudek candidate vision.

## Public visual grammar
- Current public pages use a clean, high-whitespace enterprise product system with bright blue as the primary recognition color, dark charcoal text, pale blue-gray surfaces, rounded controls, and strong product screenshots.
- Source-sampled campaign tokens: DeepHow blue `#4388F7`, deeper interaction blue `#2167D8`, charcoal `#252A34`, white, and pale cloud surfaces. Exact source CSS was not copied; values are conservative visual samples from the public identity and logo.
- Candidate-original coordinate-instrument accents (cyan, amber, violet, red) are subordinate and used only to explain the role thesis.

## Typography
- The public brand presents a modern geometric/grotesk sans character. Proprietary font binaries were not copied.
- Inter Display / Inter are used as licensed structural substitutes available in the publication environment. The decision preserves the public character while avoiding unlicensed assets.

## Imagery
- No company photography is committed in this publication package. The candidate experience uses original role-derived diagrams and the unmodified official DeepHow logo retrieved by the publication workflow.
- The omission avoids fragile hotlinks and keeps the campaign focused on the platform thesis rather than implying ownership of pictured customer or product outcomes.

## Artifact application
- Website: official logo above the fold, DeepHow recognition blue, product image, and independent-candidate label.
- Resume and cover letter: restrained blue rule, employer logo lockup, one shared professional header, and no imitation corporate letterhead.
- Brief, plan, and Work Address Review: same typography and restrained token system; role-derived coordinate-system diagrams remain subordinate to the official identity.
