Candidate operating artifact · 01

Give every physical-AI use case a platform address before giving it a roadmap slot.

A compact review for deciding whether a customer problem should use existing platform contracts, extend them, remain federated, or stop.

1 · Frame the work

FieldReview prompt
Customer annoyanceWhat concrete repeated friction is occurring in the actual workflow—not only the executive goal?
Performed unit of workWhat action, step, output, cycle, or decision is the platform trying to understand or influence?
Outcome and baselineWhat customer outcome should move, in what unit, against what current baseline or clearly labeled assumption?
Users and authorityWho performs, supervises, configures, reviews, owns, and can override the workflow?
EnvironmentWhich site, station, asset, product, conditions, languages, camera/hardware, and network constraints matter?

2 · Resolve the five coordinates

CoordinateRequired definitionMinimum evidence
Work identityProcess, role, asset, product, site, SOP/versionStable identifiers and approved source
Expected actionSteps, sequence, tolerances, output, permitted variationExpert-approved standard
Observed executionVideo, photo, event, timing, motion, contextRepresentative and legally usable data
Evidence contractConfidence, latency, error posture, exception, human authority, recoveryEvaluation set and decision owner
Learning returnVariation, override, outcome, drift, SOP/model changeInstrumentation and review cadence
