The real mandate

Turn four product lines into one compounding platform.

DeepHow is moving from knowledge capture into verified execution and operational intelligence. The Product Director has to create shared architecture without slowing product-line learning or customer value.

Platform vs. solution

Standardize the work object, evidence model, security, APIs, and evaluation system—while preserving legitimate industry and customer variation.

Speed vs. trust

Run fast in-market experiments, but set confidence, latency, fallback, and human-authority thresholds that survive the factory floor.

Enterprise vs. end user

Meet CIO, IT, AI, security, and governance requirements while removing the frontline annoyances that determine daily adoption.

Commitment vs. leverage

Honor near-term customer commitments without turning every strategic account into a permanent fork of the platform.

Compress the path from first access to first verified value—then make each deployment strengthen the platform.

Original operating model

One work address. Five coordinates. Four product surfaces.

A performed unit of work becomes the common product object. Every capability reads or enriches the same address rather than creating a separate data island.

1

Work identity

Process, station, asset, product, role, site, SOP version, and approved context.

2

Expected action

Steps, sequence, tolerances, job aids, expected output, and permitted variation.

3

Observed execution

Video, photo, events, timing, motion, environment, and operator interaction.

4

Evidence contract

Confidence, latency, precision/recall posture, exception class, human review, and recovery.

5

Learning return

Variation, overrides, defects, cycle time, drift, SOP change, and reusable improvement.

Platform substrateOntology and event model · APIs and service boundaries · identity and access · security and governance · model/evaluation lifecycle · observability · data rights and retention
Product judgmentStandardize what makes work legible and trusted. Federate what genuinely varies by industry, site, workflow, model, or risk.
Commercial leverageDesign the address once; activate additional product surfaces with less integration effort and clearer expansion value.

Product-line coherence

The products stay distinct. The work becomes shared.

Each product line creates a different customer outcome, but all should resolve to the same underlying work identity, evidence vocabulary, and enterprise controls.

KCT

Knowledge Capture & Transfer

Preserve expert know-how and turn it into usable, versioned guidance.

  • Creates expected-action context
  • Anchors role, asset, and SOP version
  • Feeds retrieval and instruction
OOV

Operational Output Verification

Compare completed work against an evidence standard using photo or video.

  • Defines output evidence
  • Sets tolerance and exception policy
  • Returns verified result
LSV

Live SOP Verification

Reason over actions and sequence while the work is happening.

  • Requires latency-aware thresholds
  • Detects procedural drift
  • Supports immediate intervention
TMA

Time & Motion AI

Classify activity, variation, and cycle time to improve flow.

  • Measures work across time
  • Surfaces waste and imbalance
  • Returns improvement hypotheses

120-day entry plan

Read the work. Define the address. Prove the leverage. Install the cadence.

A director-level entry sequence that balances customer commitments with durable platform architecture.

Days 0–30

Read

  • Map product, data, model, and integration boundaries
  • Interview frontline users, builders, customers, GTM, and engineering
  • Trace first-access to first-value journeys
  • Rank recurring annoyances and platform forks
Days 31–60

Define

  • Draft canonical work-address schema
  • Clarify standardize/federate principles
  • Set AI threshold and human-authority taxonomy
  • Reframe roadmap around outcomes and reuse
Days 61–90

Prove

  • Run bounded in-market experiments
  • Instrument activation, trust, use, and outcome
  • Test builder experience and integration contracts
  • Formalize only evidence-backed hypotheses
Days 91–120

Install

  • Launch monthly platform scorecard
  • Publish decision rights and roadmap narrative
  • Align GTM and QBR product story
  • Establish architecture, evaluation, and learning cadence
