Purpose: decide whether an AI-enabled workflow should remain matter-specific, become a practice pattern, enter cross-practice testing, become a maintained firm standard, be repaired, or retire. This is an operating metaphor, not legal authority.

1. Fact pattern

Attorney task, client objective, eligible users, matter conditions, consequence, current friction.

2. Knowledge record

Approved sources, provenance, taxonomy, access, privilege boundary, freshness, content owner.

3. Human authority

AI role, required lawyer judgment, review checkpoint, escalation, override, prohibited action.

4. Evaluation record

Representative test set, task quality, citations, omissions, agreement, security, failure and release evidence.

5. Adoption and ownership

Practice sponsor, workflow insertion point, training, support, feedback, technical and content owners.

6. Value evidence

Observed effect on matter execution, attorney leverage, quality, client responsiveness, or economics.
